History

Escuela Secundaria Técnica México (ESTM) was established as a gift from the Government of Mexico to Belize, aiming to provide quality education with a focus on both academic and technical skills. Located in San Roman Village, Corozal District, the school was founded to support Belize’s educational development by equipping students with practical knowledge in vocational and business fields. Over the years, ESTM has expanded its facilities and programs to accommodate a growing student population, ensuring that learners receive a well-rounded education that prepares them for future careers. The school has also engaged in community initiatives, such as radiothons to raise funds for the ESTM Ambassadors Marching Band, further demonstrating its commitment to academic excellence and student success.

Mission Statement

Escuela Secundaria Técnica México will provide quality education, promote knowledge, and develop skills and positive attitudes in the vocational/technical and academic/business fields through theoretical and practical applications of learning to prepare productive citizens in society.

Our Vision For The Future

Escuela Secundaria Técnica México (ESTM) envisions becoming a leading institution in technical and academic education in Belize, empowering students with the skills, knowledge, and values needed to excel in an evolving world. The school aims to integrate advanced technology, expand vocational training programs, and foster innovation to prepare students for diverse career paths. Through strong community engagement, modernized facilities, and a commitment to excellence, ESTM strives to nurture responsible, skilled, and forward-thinking individuals who will contribute to the development of Belize and beyond.
